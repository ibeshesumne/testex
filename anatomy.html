Strength-Training Anatomy Reference

Region-by-region listing of the muscles, joints, and main movements that matter for active resistance training, with links to clear, freely-licensed annotated diagrams.

The structure follows the ten plates in the photographed book (anatomy_neck.jpeg … anatomy_calf.jpeg). For each region you get:

  1. Key muscles trained (with the heads/parts that are programmed independently in lifting).
  2. Joints involved and the movements they perform.
  3. Primary lifts that load those structures.
  4. Free annotated diagrams — labeled, high-resolution, downloadable.

1. Neck

Muscles trained

  • Sternocleidomastoid (flexion, contralateral rotation)
  • Trapezius — upper fibers (also a shoulder muscle; elevates scapula and extends the neck)
  • Splenius capitis & cervicis (extension, ipsilateral rotation)
  • Semispinalis capitis & cervicis
  • Longissimus capitis & cervicis
  • Levator scapulae
  • Scalenes (anterior, middle, posterior — accessory respiration, lateral flexion)
  • Deep prevertebrals: longus colli, longus capitis, rectus capitis anterior/lateralis
  • Suprahyoids / infrahyoids (postural support during heavy bracing)
  • Platysma (superficial)

Joints

  • Atlanto-occipital (C0–C1) — head nodding (flexion / extension)
  • Atlanto-axial (C1–C2) — head rotation (~50% of cervical rotation)
  • Cervical facet & intervertebral joints C2–C7 — flexion, extension, lateral flexion, rotation

Primary lifts

Neck harness flexion / extension, plate-on-forehead lying neck flexion, prone neck extension, lateral neck raises, “wrestler’s bridge” (advanced), shrugs (upper trap overlap).

2. Shoulder (pectoral girdle + glenohumeral)

Muscles trained

  • Deltoid — anterior, lateral (middle), posterior heads
  • Rotator cuff — Supraspinatus, Infraspinatus, Teres minor, Subscapularis (the “SITS” cuff)
  • Teres major
  • Trapezius — upper / middle / lower fibers
  • Rhomboid major & minor
  • Levator scapulae
  • Serratus anterior (scapular protraction & upward rotation — critical for overhead pressing)
  • Pectoralis minor (scapular depression / anterior tilt)
  • Subclavius
  • Coracobrachialis

Joints

  • Glenohumeral — flexion, extension, abduction, adduction, internal & external rotation, horizontal ad/abduction
  • Acromioclavicular (AC) — gliding & rotation of scapula
  • Sternoclavicular (SC) — only true bony link of the upper limb to the axial skeleton
  • Scapulothoracic (functional joint) — protraction, retraction, elevation, depression, upward / downward rotation

Primary lifts

Overhead press (barbell / dumbbell / push press), Arnold press, lateral raise, front raise, rear-delt fly / reverse pec-deck, face pull, upright row, scaption raise, rotator-cuff external rotations, landmine press, handstand press.

3. Chest

Muscles trained

  • Pectoralis major — clavicular (upper), sternocostal (middle), abdominal (lower) heads
  • Pectoralis minor
  • Serratus anterior (overlaps shoulder; works on every press)
  • Subclavius
  • External & internal intercostals (stabilizers; visible on the plate but not directly programmed)

Joints

  • Glenohumeral (horizontal adduction is the main pec action)
  • Sternoclavicular & acromioclavicular (move with every press)
  • Sternocostal & costochondral, costovertebral (rib-cage articulations that allow chest expansion under load)

Primary lifts

Bench press (flat / incline / decline), dumbbell press, dumbbell fly, cable crossover, pec-deck, push-up variations, dips (chest-biased forward lean), landmine press, svend press, pullover (overlap with lats).

4. Back

Muscles trained (superficial → deep)

  • Trapezius (upper, middle, lower)
  • Latissimus dorsi
  • Rhomboid major & minor
  • Levator scapulae
  • Serratus posterior superior & inferior
  • Erector spinae group: Iliocostalis, Longissimus, Spinalis (cervicis, thoracis, lumborum)
  • Transversospinalis group: Semispinalis, Multifidus, Rotatores
  • Interspinales, Intertransversarii (deep segmental)
  • Quadratus lumborum
  • Thoracolumbar fascia (force transmitter — landmark)

Joints

  • Cervical, thoracic, lumbar facet (zygapophyseal) joints
  • Intervertebral disc joints (cartilaginous) C2–S1
  • Costovertebral & costotransverse (thoracic only)
  • Sacroiliac (load transfer pelvis ↔︎ spine)
  • Lumbosacral junction (L5–S1)

Primary lifts

Deadlift (conventional, sumo, Romanian, stiff-leg, trap-bar), bent-over row, Pendlay row, T-bar row, seated cable row, single-arm DB row, chest-supported row, pull-up / chin-up, lat pulldown, straight-arm pulldown, shrug, rack pull, good morning, back extension / hyperextension, reverse hyper, farmer carry, suitcase carry.

5. Biceps / anterior upper arm

Muscles trained

  • Biceps brachii — long head (lateral; crosses shoulder, biased by incline / overhead curls) and short head (medial; biased by preacher / spider curls)
  • Brachialis (deep to biceps; main elbow flexor at any forearm position — trained heavily by hammer / reverse curls)
  • Brachioradialis (lateral forearm; elbow flexor with neutral grip)
  • Coracobrachialis (assists shoulder flexion & adduction)

Joints

  • Glenohumeral (long head crosses it — relevant to incline curls and cable curl behind the body)
  • Humeroulnar + humeroradial (the elbow hinge — flexion / extension)
  • Proximal radioulnar (pivot — supination during a barbell curl)

Primary lifts

Barbell curl, EZ-bar curl, dumbbell curl, incline DB curl, preacher curl, Scott curl, spider curl, hammer curl, Zottman curl, cable curl, concentration curl, drag curl, chin-up (bodyweight biceps work).

6. Triceps / posterior upper arm

Muscles trained

  • Triceps brachii
    • Long head — origin on infraglenoid tubercle of scapula; crosses the shoulder, so it’s biased by overhead extensions
    • Lateral head — biased by pronated-grip pressdowns
    • Medial head — works at every angle; biased by close-grip / bench movements
  • Anconeus (small elbow extensor / stabilizer)

Joints

  • Glenohumeral (long head only — overhead extensions emphasize it)
  • Humeroulnar + humeroradial (elbow extension)

Primary lifts

Close-grip bench press, dip (triceps-biased upright torso), overhead triceps extension (DB / EZ-bar / cable rope), lying triceps extension (“skullcrusher”), triceps pressdown (rope / bar / V-handle), kickback, JM press, board press, tate press, diamond push-up.

7. Forearm

Muscles trained

  • Anterior (flexor) compartment
    • Pronator teres
    • Flexor carpi radialis
    • Palmaris longus (absent in ~15% of people)
    • Flexor carpi ulnaris
    • Flexor digitorum superficialis
    • Flexor digitorum profundus
    • Flexor pollicis longus
    • Pronator quadratus
  • Posterior (extensor) compartment
    • Brachioradialis
    • Extensor carpi radialis longus & brevis
    • Extensor digitorum
    • Extensor digiti minimi
    • Extensor carpi ulnaris
    • Supinator
    • Abductor pollicis longus
    • Extensor pollicis brevis & longus
    • Extensor indicis

Joints

  • Humeroulnar + humeroradial (elbow)
  • Proximal & distal radioulnar — pronation / supination
  • Radiocarpal (wrist) + midcarpal — flexion, extension, radial & ulnar deviation
  • Carpometacarpal (esp. thumb saddle joint), MCP, PIP, DIP — grip

Primary lifts

Wrist curl & reverse wrist curl (DB / barbell), wrist roller, behind- the-back wrist curl, Zottman curl, hammer curl, reverse curl, pinch- grip plate hold, dead-hang from bar, farmer / suitcase carry, towel pull-up, fat-grip work, captains-of-crush gripper.

8. Abdominals / anterior trunk

Muscles trained

  • Rectus abdominis (the “six-pack”; trunk flexion, posterior pelvic tilt)
  • External oblique (contralateral rotation, lateral flexion)
  • Internal oblique (ipsilateral rotation, lateral flexion)
  • Transversus abdominis (deep corset; intra-abdominal pressure for bracing under heavy lifts)
  • Pyramidalis (small, tenses linea alba)
  • Quadratus lumborum (lateral flexion, hip hike — posterior wall)
  • Diaphragm (primary breathing muscle, key to the Valsalva brace)
  • Iliopsoas (psoas major, psoas minor, iliacus — strong hip flexors; shown on every front plate but functionally a hip muscle)
  • Landmarks: linea alba, rectus sheath, inguinal ligament

Joints

  • Thoracolumbar intervertebral & facet joints (flexion, lateral flexion, rotation)
  • Lumbosacral joint (L5–S1)
  • Sacroiliac joints (force transfer)
  • Pubic symphysis

Primary lifts

Crunch, sit-up, hanging leg raise, hanging knee raise, toes-to-bar, ab-wheel rollout, cable crunch, decline sit-up, Russian twist, Pallof press, suitcase / farmer carry (anti-lateral-flexion), side plank, plank, dead bug, hollow-body hold, weighted hyperextension. Bracing for squat / deadlift / press is itself an abdominal exercise.

9. Thigh / hip

Muscles trained

  • Anterior — quadriceps + hip flexors
    • Rectus femoris (crosses hip & knee)
    • Vastus lateralis, vastus medialis (incl. VMO), vastus intermedius
    • Sartorius
    • Iliopsoas (psoas major + iliacus), pectineus
    • Tensor fasciae latae → iliotibial band
  • Medial — adductor group
    • Adductor longus, adductor brevis
    • Adductor magnus (has a large posterior hamstring portion)
    • Gracilis
    • Obturator externus
  • Posterior — hamstrings
    • Biceps femoris (long head crosses hip; short head only crosses knee)
    • Semitendinosus
    • Semimembranosus
  • Gluteal & deep hip rotators
    • Gluteus maximus (extension, external rotation; major hip extensor)
    • Gluteus medius & minimus (abduction & pelvic stability)
    • Piriformis, superior & inferior gemellus, obturator internus, quadratus femoris (deep external rotators)

Joints

  • Hip (acetabulofemoral) — flexion / extension, abduction / adduction, internal / external rotation, circumduction
  • Tibiofemoral (knee) — flexion / extension + small rotation when flexed
  • Patellofemoral (the patella–trochlea joint; loaded heavily in squats and leg extension)
  • Sacroiliac, pubic symphysis

Primary lifts

Back squat (high- & low-bar), front squat, overhead squat, box squat, goblet squat, split squat / Bulgarian split squat, lunge (forward, reverse, walking), leg press, hack squat, sissy squat, leg extension, Romanian deadlift, stiff-leg deadlift, conventional & sumo deadlift, good morning, leg curl (lying / seated / standing), Nordic ham curl, glute–ham raise, hip thrust, glute bridge, cable kickback, hip abduction / adduction machine, Copenhagen plank, step-up.

10. Calf / lower leg

Muscles trained

  • Posterior, superficial (the calf bulk)
    • Gastrocnemius — medial & lateral heads (crosses the knee; biased by standing calf raises with the knee straight)
    • Soleus (does not cross the knee; biased by seated calf raises)
    • Plantaris (small, often vestigial)
    • Common insertion via the Achilles (calcaneal) tendon
  • Posterior, deep
    • Popliteus (unlocks the knee)
    • Tibialis posterior
    • Flexor digitorum longus
    • Flexor hallucis longus
  • Lateral
    • Fibularis (peroneus) longus & brevis
  • Anterior (shin)
    • Tibialis anterior
    • Extensor digitorum longus
    • Extensor hallucis longus
    • Fibularis tertius

Joints

  • Tibiofemoral (knee — gastrocnemius crosses it)
  • Proximal & distal tibiofibular
  • Talocrural (ankle) — plantarflexion / dorsiflexion
  • Subtalar — inversion / eversion
  • Transverse tarsal, tarsometatarsal, MTP, IP — toe loading

Primary lifts

Standing calf raise (machine / Smith / barbell), seated calf raise, donkey calf raise, leg-press calf raise, single-leg DB calf raise, tibialis raise (anti-shin work), farmer-walk on toes, jump rope, heavy ankle dorsiflexion (banded). Calves also get loaded in every squat, deadlift, lunge, and carry.

Quick cross-reference: which lift hits which region

Lift Primary region(s) loaded
Back squat Thigh (quads, glutes, adductors), abdominals, back (erectors), calf
Deadlift Back (lats, traps, erectors), thigh (glutes, hams), forearm (grip), abdominals
Bench press Chest, triceps, shoulder (anterior delt)
Overhead press Shoulder, triceps, abdominals (bracing), back (upper traps)
Pull-up / row Back (lats, rhomboids, mid-trap), biceps, forearm
Romanian deadlift Thigh (hamstrings, glutes), back (erectors), forearm
Hip thrust Thigh (glutes, hams)
Standing calf raise Calf (gastrocnemius-biased)
Seated calf raise Calf (soleus-biased)
Curl variations Biceps, brachialis, forearm
Triceps extension Triceps (long head if overhead)
Wrist curl / wrist roller Forearm
Plank / rollout / Pallof Abdominals (anti-extension / anti-rotation)
Neck harness work Neck
