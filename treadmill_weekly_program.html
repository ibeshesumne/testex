Weekly Treadmill Program

A balanced week that combines HIIT, incline walking, and endurance work to maximize calorie burn, cardiovascular progress, and recovery. Most hard sessions stay in the 15-30 minute range; endurance sessions can run 45+ minutes when desired.

2-3 HIIT sessions/week Incline + intervals Warm-up + cool-down every session

7-Day Schedule

Day Session Main Work Total Time
Monday HIIT Pyramid 30s sprint/30s walk, 1 min run/1 min walk, 2 min run/1 min walk, 3 min run/1 min walk, 4 min run/1 min walk, then descend back down. ~30 min
Tuesday 12-3-30 Incline Walk 12% incline at 3 mph for 30 minutes (steady, no handrail support). ~40 min (incl. warm-up/cool-down)
Wednesday 3-2-1 Endurance Intervals Repeat: 3 min fast run, 2 min jog, 1 min walk for 20-30 minutes. ~30-40 min
Thursday Low-Impact Incline Intervals 10-minute set of 25-second pushes at 5-9% incline and 3.5-4 mph with easier recovery periods. ~20-30 min
Friday HIIT Hill Sprints 3-4 rounds: 2 min hard run at 5-10% incline, then 1 min walk recovery. ~20-30 min
Saturday Steady State Incline Jog 20-40 min moderate jog, adding 0.5% incline every 5 minutes. ~30-50 min
Sunday Recovery / Optional Walk Rest day or easy walk + mobility work to improve recovery for the next week. 20-30 min optional

Session Library (swap options)

Use these to rotate workouts while keeping the same weekly structure.

15-Minute Stamina Booster

  • 1 min walk
  • 1 min easy jog with 20-second speed pickups
  • 6 rounds: 30 sec jog / 15 sec sprint
  • 45 sec walk recovery between rounds

Incline Pyramid

  • Increase incline by 1% every 1-2 minutes
  • Peak around 10-12%
  • Decrease by 1% every 1-2 minutes back down

HIIT Hill Sprint

  • 3-4 rounds total
  • 2 min hard run at 5-10% incline
  • 1 min walk recovery

12-3-30 Baseline

  • 12% incline
  • 3 mph
  • 30 minutes continuous walking

Execution Rules

  • Warm-up/cool-down: 5 minutes easy walk or jog before and after hard sessions.
  • Recovery: Keep HIIT to 2-3 days per week; use incline/tempo work between intense days.
  • Form: Avoid holding handrails to preserve training effect and natural gait mechanics.
  • Progression: Increase speed, incline, or rounds gradually (one variable at a time each week).
